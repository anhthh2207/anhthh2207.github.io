1. Reactive Methods

Reactive methods utilize real-time sensor data to generate immediate responses without complex planning. They are sensitive to noise and often suboptimal in complex environments.

Wall-following

The PID wall-following algorithm keeps a fixed distance to the wall using a PID controller.

PID wall-following

While simple, it requires extensive PID tuning and struggles with high speeds or sharp turns.

Gap-following

This method identifies the largest gaps in LIDAR scans and drives towards them.

Gap-following in obstacle-free map

Gap-following in obstacle map

Gap-following achieves smoother navigation and better obstacle avoidance, but still lacks trajectory optimality.

2. Planning-based Methods

These methods improve performance by utilizing prior environmental knowledge or planned paths.

Pure Pursuit

Pure pursuit uses a look-ahead point on a pre-defined trajectory for control.

Pure pursuit with SLAM-estimated map

Enhanced pure pursuit with trajectory smoothing

Despite its simplicity, pure pursuit enables fast, stable, and effective path tracking, especially when paired with advanced speed control strategies.
